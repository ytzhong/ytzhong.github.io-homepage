Team Science

Collaborative research

I currently have limited room available for new collaborators, with priority given to projects targeting childhood cancer or cancer research more broadly. If you have a project in mind, feel free to reach out.

What I can offer collaborators

Assist with grant application

I have led both nationally funded grants (e.g., NIH) and foundation-funded awards as principal investigator. I can contribute at every stage from concept and specific aims through analysis and reporting. I can either lead or serve as a statistical co-investigator.

Statistical genetics expertise

Design and analysis for genome-wide association studies (GWAS) and post-GWAS analysis. Input on sampling, power and sample-size calculations, confounding, and population structure.

High-dimensional genomics and integrative data analysis

Data processing and statistical methods for large-scale genomic, transcriptomic, and phenotypic data.

Method development tailored to your data

Building or adapting statistical methods when off-the-shelf tools don't fit the study design or data structure.

Trainee involvement

Connecting collaborative projects with mentored students or staff for analysis support.

How to request collaboration

Statistical support

To request statistical support, please visit the University of Minnesota CTSI Biostatistical Support page and follow the consultation request link. For general inquiries, contact ctsi@umn.edu — or email me directly at yang3704@umn.edu.

Grant assistance

For grant assistance, please refer to the effort-level guidance widely accepted in the biostatistics community — Duke's guidance on effort estimation for collaborative biostatisticians.
